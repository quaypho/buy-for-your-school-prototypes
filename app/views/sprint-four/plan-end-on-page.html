Tasks to help understand the needs and goals for the catering service.

Understand the performance of the existing supplier and contract.

Find out from {{ data["domain-expert"] }} what the existing contract offers and what it has been like working with that supplier. You may find it useful to focus on what has gone well and what can be improved?

The aim of this step is to help you articulate your goal for this procurement in the specification builder. You can find some guidance on this at www.gov.uk/buy-for-your-school/understanding-exisiting-contracts.html.

Review the finances for the current contract

Catering contracts can be cost neutral or turn a profit for the school. Find out about the different types of catering contracts available at www.gov.uk/buy-for-your-school/different-types-of-catering-contracts.html.

You may also want to consider bringing catering in house, although this can be a time consuming process. More information can be found here www.gov.uk/buy-for-your-school/different-types-of-catering-contracts.html#in-house-catering.

Find out what staff, governors and students want from a new catering service

You may want to speak to the headteacher, governors and parent teachers association to find out what they want from a new catering contract. This may also help identify what key performance indicators (KPIs) you can use to monitor the new contract.

It may be useful to ask the students what they think of the current catering services and what their needs are. This can be done through a student survey. More information can be found here www.gov.uk/buy-for-your-school/guidance/school-survey.html.

Understand the timeline

You identified that the new catering service needs to be in place and ready by 6 September 2021. You will need to give notice on your current contract, find out what the notice period is and give notice at the appropriate time.

Contracts for complex services usually change during the easter or summer holidays, as the services will usually not be required during these periods. It also gives time to deal with any unforeseen circumstances.

You may also want to start thinking about how you want to transition from the old suppliers to the new.

Equipment and facilities audit

Catering suppliers will need to know what equipment and facilities will be available to them. Understanding what the school and the current supplier can be complicated. You have identified Peter Hallam as the person to speak to about the current contract. You may also want to speak to the current supplier or locate the current contrac

New suppliers for catering may want to visit the school so that they can see the facilities and better understand the situation

Some existing equipment many need disposing of, this can be something that the new supplier helps with.

Review standards and legislation

You need to understand what your obligations are and what they mean for your school. Find out where you can learn about relevant legislation. This may be from:

  • www.gov.uk/catering-legislation
  • Speaking to the school’s buyer professional network
  • Suppliers
  • Public sector buying organisations
  • School’s buying hubs

Identify ethical and environmental considerations

Your school may have policies or considerations regarding the ethical and environmental impact of the service you are looking to procure. The head teacher or governors may be able to help you understand these policies/considerations so that you can include them in the specification you will need to share with suppliers.

Find out if there are regional policies that will affect you procurement

Be aware that there could be local policies that might apply to your school. We recommend speaking to your local authority or school’s buyer professional network to see if you are subject to any local policies.

Additional contract requirements

There may be additional requirements for your catering contract outside of what you would consider business as usual. Your existing catering contract and Peter Hallam may be able to help you identify these requirements.

They can include:

  • Special dietary requirements or allergies
  • Additional meals required during a normal school day
  • Meals required on school trips or open days
  • Cultural considerations
  • Only paying for what is consumed
